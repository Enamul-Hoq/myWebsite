About Me

I am Md Enamul Hoq, a 3rd year PhD student at UAMS, specializing in Medical Quantitative Imaging and AI. My academic journey has been fueled by a profound interest in bridging the realms of physics, artificial intelligence, and medical imaging to develop innovative solutions for healthcare.

My research focuses on designing and implementing a foundation model for lung cancer CT images, aiming to enhance early detection and diagnostic accuracy. This work involves the integration of advanced AI algorithms with comprehensive imaging data, contributing to the evolution of personalized medicine.

Beyond the technical aspects, I am passionate about the potential of interdisciplinary research to transform patient outcomes. By leveraging the principles of physics and the power of AI, my goal is to pave the way for groundbreaking advancements in medical imaging and diagnostics.

In addition to my research, I actively engage in academic collaborations, presenting my findings at various international conferences, and contributing to peer-reviewed journals. My commitment to continuous learning and innovation drives my ambition to make a significant impact in the field of medical imaging and beyond.
